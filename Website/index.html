Welcome to Project LIRIUS

LIRIUS is Conestoga College’s student-led CubeSat initiative under the Conestoga Engineering Society. Our mission is to design and launch a 3U nanosatellite focused on advancing climate research and providing hands-on space systems engineering experience.

Mission Overview

Operating in Low Earth Orbit (400–500 km), LIRIUS aims to investigate the link between atmospheric methane emissions, noctilucent cloud (PMC) formation, and plasma density variations in the thermosphere. The satellite is equipped with a Langmuir probe and high-resolution imaging payload to explore this under-researched correlation, contributing data to improve climate models and space weather prediction.

Scientific & Educational Goals

  • Study plasma density and PMC variation to identify signs of mesospheric cooling due to methane emissions.
  • Correlate environmental data with Earth-observing satellites like TROPOMI and GHGSat.
  • Train students in satellite design, systems integration, and regulatory compliance.
  • Contribute to Canada's environmental research and sustainable space initiatives.
